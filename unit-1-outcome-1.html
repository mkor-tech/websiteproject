The regulation has a number of aims all designed to increase and standardise the protection of individuals including:

  • Data portability - The right to data portability allows individuals to obtain and reuse their personal data for their own purposes across different services.
  • The right to be forgotten - The GDPR introduces a right for individuals to have personal data erased. The right to erasure is also known as ‘the right to be forgotten’.
  • The right to prevent profiling - The GDPR gives people the right not to be subject to solely automated decisions, including profiling, which have a legal or similarly significant effect on them.
  • The right to object to processing - Individuals have an absolute right to stop their data being used for direct marketing.
  • The right to rectification and erasure - The GDPR includes a right for individuals to have inaccurate personal data rectified, or completed if it is incomplete.

Data protection is about ensuring people can trust you to use their data fairly and responsibly. This might be anyone, including a customer, client, employee, partner, member, supporter, business contact, public official or member of the public.
